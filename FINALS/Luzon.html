VISAYAS MINDANAO

The Puerto-Princesa Subterranean River National Park contains one of the world's most impressive cave systems, with spectacular limestone karst landscapes, pristine natural beauty, intact old-growth forests, and unique wildlife.

The Banaue Rice Terraces (Filipino: Hagdan-hagdang Palayan ng Banawe) are terraces carved into the mountains of Banaue, Ifugao, by the Igorot people's ancestors. The terraces are sometimes referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World."
Mount Pulag (also known as Mount Pulog) is the Philippines' third highest peak. At 2,922 meters above sea level, it is Luzon's highest point. At the mountain's peak, the provinces of Benguet, Ifugao, and Nueva Vizcaya meet.
Intramuros, also known as the "Walled City," is a place steeped in history and culture. With its cobblestone streets and preserved architecture dating back hundreds of years, you'll wonder if you're still in the Philippines.
